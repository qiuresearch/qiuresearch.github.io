Crystal field effect

Mn ions sit in the center of the octahedral cage formed by six O2- ions. Let us first assume that the octahedral cage has normal shape, meaning six equi-distant Mn-O bond lengths. As a consequence, the spherical symmetry of the Mn ions is broken; the degeneracy of the five l = 2 3d orbitals is also broken. This is known as the crystal field effect, though most times only the influence of neighboring O2- ions is considered. Considering the cubic symmetry of the surrounding O2- ions, the split of the five d-shell orbitals results in two groups, the bonding orbitals dxy, dyz, dzx with symmetry class t2g, and the bonding orbitals d3z2-r2, dx2-y2 with symmetry class eg. The five 3d orbitals are visualized in Fig. 3.3. eg orbitals have higher energy than t2g orbitals. This can be understood qualitatively as electrons in eg orbitals feel more repulsion from the O2- ions than in t2g orbitals (eg orbitals project toward neighboring O2- ions, while t2g orbitals points into the empty space in between O2- ions).

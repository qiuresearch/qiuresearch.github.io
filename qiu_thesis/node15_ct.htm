Use of the image plate as an X-ray detector

The detection media of an image plate (IP) is one layer of very small crystalline grains of photo-stimulable phosphor mixed with organic binders. The grain size is about 5 m; the layer thickness is usually around 150 m. There is usually a protective layer (about 10 m) on the top and one support layer on the bottom (about 250 m). A polyester backing plate is often used to make it reusable and easy to handle as a flexible plastic plate. The IP's sensitive surface can be easily identified by its all-blue-white or all-white appearance, while the backing side is usually black or gray.

Outline of this thesis

Investigation of the local structure employing the PDF technique is the the central theme of this thesis work, covering both technical developments and scientific applications.

Chapter 2 will describe a recent development of a new way to collect PDF data: rapid acquisition pair distribution function (RA-PDF) method, where the coupling of high energy X-ray with an area detector leads to three to four orders of magnitude decrease of data collection time.

Chapters 3 and 4 focus on the high resolution PDF analysis of neutron powder diffraction data on two manganite systems: undoped LaMnO3 and bi-layered La2-2xSr1+2xMn2O7, respectively. The significance of local Jahn-Teller distortions will be emphasized, and their strong influence on the electronic, and magnetic degrees of freedom will be discussed.

Chapter 5 will demonstrate a few applications of the RA-PDF method, e.g., resolving the distorted square Ti nets in Ti2Sb, and the in-situ chemical reduction of CuO to Cu.

Chapter 6 summarizes the thesis and discusses some future work.

Appendix A describes the experimental aspects and post-data processing of the RA-PDF method in detail.

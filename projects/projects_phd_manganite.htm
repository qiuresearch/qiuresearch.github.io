CMR Manganite

      Crystalline materials possess long range periodic atomic structures, which are routinely solved with conventional crystallography. With the increasing complexity of modern materials, local deviations from this perfect long range ordering often appear to be vital to its function and properties. Local structure determination methods are available to probe the neighboring atomic arrangements, e.g., the atomic pair distribution function (PDF) method. However, the intermediate structure at nano-meter scale presents great challenge, noting its importance to the structure of nanocrystals. We are exploring the total scattering PDF method to fill this void. This work also gives an example of nano-scale intermediate range structure in a highly crystalline material.
Figure 1: Crystal structure of LaMnO3. Mn atom is in the center of the octahedron of Oxygen atoms.

      The family of manganites is known for its potentially technologically important colossal magneto-resistive effect. The parent undoped compound, LaMnO3, takes on a corner shared MnO6 octahedra network, with Mn atoms in the octahedral center. This seemingly simple structure nevertheless proves to be puzzling at high temperatures where the local and average structure approaches disagree. While distorted MnO6 octahedron is evident from the local structure analysis, the average structure indicates an octahedron with six nearly equal Mn-O bonds. The PDF method, being a total scattering technique, is capable of probing the structure at multiple length scales. We applied the PDF method to resolve the discrepancy. Our PDF analysis first confirmed the earlier local and average structure results. Then, the question is how the locally distorted octahedron would appear undistorted on average. The obvious answer is that the octahedral orientations are random in long range. The next important question is whether the octahedral orientation has any intermediate range ordering. Through a novel PDF varying range analysis, we for the first time found that there is nano-scale intermediate range ordering of the distorted MnO6 octahedra, thus reconciled the disagreeing pictures in local and average structure. The length scale of ths intermediate range ordering is about 1.6 nm.

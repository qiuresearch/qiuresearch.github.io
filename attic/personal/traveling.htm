Traveling

Cincinnati, July, 2003

   On the occasion of 2003 ACA meeting, I visited the city of Cincinnati. Two things I enjoyed most, one is the bridges over the Ohio river (?), the other is my first Major League Baseball (MLB) game experience.
   As more and more addicting to running, I couldn't resist the temptation running across the four bridges riding on the Ohio river. Eventually, on the day before my leave, this contrived plot was put into action. It turned out that I was over optimistic about my running caliber. The lunch break I set aside for this event fell much short of painful journey it actually took. But anyway, I survived, sweating like a turkey, dragging my legs with a tail in between. Later, I concluded that the killer was the considerable latitude changes when up and down the bridges, which in fact needs rather large amount of work. :)
   Taking advantage of the free ticket from my boss, I went to see my first MLB game with great anticipation. Even warned about the ripping prices on beers and hot dogs, I still got pissed off by the watered mixed beer and only luke warm hot dogs. BUT, I enjoyed the game! With the patient explanation on the rules from my boss and another nice guy, I could go with the flow in the game with little difficulty. (am I cool?) I loved the relaxing atmosphere the most, sitting high above in a cool summer evening, getting winded up with beers and shouts and laughs, chatting with the neighbors, and you are also enjoying a great game just in front of you. It's the life style I never thought I would like, but my experience proved otherwise. After getting back to MSU, I even convinced my several buddies to watch our Lansing Lugnuts game. Ha ha!

Pictured rocks, July, 2003

   My first camping experience turned out to terrific, even though the cold night was a bit suffering. Along the trip, there were three couples and two singles. Enjoying the benefit of being single, I didn't need to worry about the pre-camping preparations. They deserve a lots of credits for making my trip so memorable. We were divided into two groups on the road. All the couples started on Friday morning, and were in charge of locating camping place, setting up the first, etc. (ha ha) Ziyu and me followed them up later in the evening. Around 10:00pm, we picked up the rental car, and my journey started right from here.
   It was about 11:30pm when we eventually hit the road; cloudless sky brought seemingly endless darkness; blurry shadows of trees and houses at distances looked unusually mystic; random lights from far on and off pushed my mind to wander; scenes nearby seemed to fall behind so fast that you couldn't even catch a glimpse. Some indescribable ecstasy filled up my whole body, the wind rushing in from the open window took my soul away with magic. I felt like catching the essence of being part of the nature and melting with it. Somehow, at that moment, one friend's words came into my mind, "I especially enjoy unplanned trips, adventures". It didn't seem there was much correlation, but I know that I wanted her to feel this too. I hoped it would never end.
   Our next stop is Soaring Eagle Casino. One hour later, we were there, thanks to very experienced Ziyu. I had thought I would never gamble, but I did anyway without knowing why. Maybe my mind was winding up somewhere. Losing money proved again to be much easier than earning. I was also sure that I wasn't enjoying gambling much, instead, I was enjoying observing a lot more. Can you believe that people never stopped showing up to abandon themselves as late as 2:00am in the morning? We said goodbye with curses around 2:30am. After two stops (one for sleeping, the other for eating), we joined our friends around 9:am near our camping place. It felt like returning home when I saw them waving hands to us beside the road.
   We stayed there one night only, had incredibly tasty barbecue with cold beers (my favorite). The boat ride along the pictured rocks is very refreshing (also my first time to apply sun cream). Standing near the edge of the deck, letting wind blowing against my face, the accumulated unhappiness and stress seemed to be gone with the wind. We also had a lot of fun playing in the water and visited the big spring. All the time, I was trying my best to establish some connection with the nature, can I really talk to it in person?
   Unexpected rain caught us on the way back, and I was insane enough to sticking my head out of the window, and got excited by the pain caused by drops of rains slamming on the face. It was just pure joy, and a bit insanity, I guess. :)
   Oh, I really want to mention that, we had chinese steamed buns for the dinner at friends' place. I loved it.

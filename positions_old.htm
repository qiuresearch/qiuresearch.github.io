A postdoctoral position is available immediately in our experimental biophysics lab at the George Washington University. Potential research projects include: 1) Structure, interaction, self-assembly, and engineering of single walled carbon nanotubes wrapped with DNA and polymers. 2) Conformation, interaction, and dynamics of nucleosome monomers and arrays. 3) Genome structure and mechanics of bacterial viruses in vitro and in vivo. 4) Intermolecular force and self-assembly of DNA with structural and functional polymers. We make use of an arsenal of experimental techniques including x-ray and neutron scattering (our lab owns a state of the art in-house x-ray setup), atomic force microscopy, optical, fluorescence, and electron microscopy.
Candidates with strong interest in biophysics and background in x-ray/neutron scattering or biopolymers are encouraged to apply. Interested applicants should send a letter of application, CV, and arrange 2-3 letters of reference to Dr. Xiangyun Qiu (xqiu@gwu.edu), 725 21st NW, Corcoran 105, Washington, DC, 20052.

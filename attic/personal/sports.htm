Sports

Tennis

   I love playing tennis, even though I am not good at it. Sports with lots of physical contacts have never attracted me a bit. A little ironically, the reason is that I hate not to give my best in competition. What happens is that I always get injured, and I am too old to afford major injuries now :).

Then, I met tennis. I really loved it. I can play as hard as I can without worrying about hurting myself or others. I also get enough workout. I have full responsibility of my own half court, which I abuse from time to time. I like playing with personality, which usually takes over winning strategies.

The bottom line is: I love this sport, and want to play better.

Running

   I can't believe that I am starting enjoying running now. :-(

I used to think of running as a very boring form of exercise. Maybe I still think it's boring now, however, I like this 'boring', if I may still call it this way. Running with rythymum and determination give me a feeling of speeding. Inexpressible calmness spreads into your soul and body. What you really wish is to never stop, even till the end of the world. I personally think it's very good way of leaving messes in daily life behind your mind, and focusing on what the real life should be about.

Swimming

   Water sports, I love all of them. I remember once a time during college, "water" was the password of all my accounts. I guess one reason is also that I can type "water" just with my left hand. :) I am not a good swimmer, a direct result of being self-taught. Sometimes, I wish I had taken a lesson so that I don't need to be surpassed frequently by the guy in the same lane. Well, I like to swim with my own ugly style.

I like to think swimming as a very relaxing sport. At least this is what I try to do in pool. I move my legs and arms wherever my mind takes them, and loosen the body. Then, just enjoy water gliding by your body, maybe rather slowly.

Yoga

   I started to practice Yoga since June 2005. I like it a lot. Yoga brings your body into a world never experienced before. The body feels rejuvenated and fresh afterwards. However, I think mind is the most important part of the practise. The mind is in total control of the body, and the body is executing the mind.

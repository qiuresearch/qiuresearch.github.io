Overview

Biological Physics
     Understanding of life at the molecular level is disturbingly crude, in contrast to the dazzling technology enjoyed by our modern materialized society. Being a fundamental question itself, knowing what life is will be unquestionably crucial to improve our lives. At first glance, the marvelous machinery of life is simply made up of finite number of molecules and presumably follows the fundamental laws of physics and chemistry that are already known. However, through millions of years of evolution, life has gained daunting complexity and molecules have developed towards perfection beyond the supposed comprehension. This certainly should not stop us from trying. We believe that indepth physical understanding at the molecular level is essential to decode the miraculous machinery.

Scientific Projects
  • Kinetics of macromolecule folding, such as RNA, protein, using time resolved small angle X-ray scattering coupled with rapid microfluidic mixers.
  • Computational fluid dynamic simulation and microfabrication of rapid microfluidic mixers for uses in fluorescence and SAXS studies of macromolecular conformational changes.


Condensed Matter Physics
      The world is getting more and more materialized faster than you can imagine. Functional materials are very diverse and have dramatically different properties, while only rather limited atomic species are frequently used. The underlying structure function relationship can not be overemphasized. My present research focuses on local structural properties of transition metal oxides.
  • Collosal magneto-resistive (CMR) manganites, high Tc cuprates, nickelates
  • (Rapid acquisition) Atomic Pair Distribution Function(PDF) technique
  • Neutron and X-ray scattering technique.
Scientific Projects
  • Local structure PDF analysis of neutron powder diffraction data of colossal magneto-resistive (CMR) manganites. Studied systems include LaMnO3, La2-2xSr1+2xMn2O7 (0.54 <=x<= 0.80). Average structure Rietveld analysis was also performed.
  • Development of the rapid acquisition pair distribution function (RA-PDF) method featuring high energy x-rays coupled with an area detector, allowing three orders of magnitude decrease of data collection time, with applications on time resolved diffraction and disordered materials.
  • Software development of PDFgetX2: a GUI driven user friendly program to obtain the PDF from x-ray powder diffraction data. Written in the Interactive Data Language (IDL), 30,000+ lines.

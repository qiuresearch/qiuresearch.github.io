Lots of arguments have been made over the consequences (or aftermaths) of the AI revolution/wave.
The optimistic camp cites the examples of previous industrial revolutions that have
mechanized agriculture and manufacturing, transformed communication and transportation,
modernized work and life, and so on. The outcomes have been enormously positive--freeing
human from laborious work, boosting physical and intellectual productivity, and unleashing
the creative ingenuities unique to the human species. AI is poised to do just the same: AI systems
will take my orders to do so many things I know how to do but find too time consuming, simply repetitve,
plainly not creative or intellectually challenging. There is a long "laundry" list: driving to places,
booking appointments, reinventing codes, proofreading and debugging, outsourcing chores, ......

Even better yet, we won't even need to move our finger or open our mouth for all of these. Perhaps
just blinking eyes, moving eyebrows, shakng heads, or facial expressions would be enough. The next step
is almost certainly for AI to decode my brain activities, so that the only effort we need to get things done
is to just think about them. We may even get tired of thinking at some point and just let AI "recommends" 
what we'd better think about. Everyone is now a superman. AI and human truly become one!
